Shadow and Vertical Sync (VSync / G-Sync / Freesync)

VSync, what is it?

VSync, Vertical Sync or VSync technology, is a graphics technology to make the image more fluid and to avoid screen tearing (left image) and stuttering (right image) by synchronising the frame rate of a game with a gaming monitor's refresh rate.

VSync allows the number of frames per second (FPS) to be limited so that the graphics card and the screen are synchronised on the rendering and display of the image.

Desynchronisation can occur when the GPU provides more images than the monitor can display.

A monitor is limited by its refresh rate. Should this rate be qual to 60Hz, the monitor will only be able to display 60 frames per second.

Please note that Shadow can go to 144Hz as said in the using automatic resolution and frame rate settings in Shadow's article. We would recommend to stick with 60Hz for resolution higher than 1920 x 1080.


G-Sync & Freesync, what's the difference?

G-Sync and Freesync are Nvidia and AMD's respective solutions to vertical synchronization.


Should we activate VSync on Shadow?

Short answer is no.

In fact, Shadow already has a built-in anti-tearing solution in its firmware.

Activating it would therefore be redundant and would not improve the quality of your image. It could actually lead to a loss of performance as resources would be used unnecessarily.

However, should you still experience screen tearing and/or stuttering, please go to our dedicated article on How to troubleshoot your internet connection.
